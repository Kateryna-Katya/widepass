A puzzle built around movement and flow.

It's not rocket science - just drag some blocks around - but somehow they made it work really well.

You basically drag around these colorful blocks that are like pieces of water pipes. By sliding and positioning these blocks, players create complete pipelines that allow water to travel through the puzzle board.

Once the correct connections are formed, water flows smoothly across the path, filling the matching containers and completing the puzzle.

When you nail it and see that water flowing perfectly? Chef's kiss. So good.

Simple rules, clever
solutions.

Every puzzle begins with a board containing several color blocks and water pipes.

You've gotta drag these blocks around the grid to make paths so the water can actually get where it needs to go.

Move one thing and suddenly everything else changes, so you can't just wing it. Some puzzles require only a few adjustments, while others demand careful planning and multiple steps ahead.

You'll need to really look at the board and think a few moves ahead - some puzzles can be tricky!

Hundreds of levels to
explore.

There's tons of levels in this game, and they slowly get trickier as you go.

Early puzzles introduce the mechanics and allow players to learn how the water flow system works. The later levels throw all sorts of new stuff at you - weird layouts, more obstacles, longer paths that'll really make you scratch your head.

Every level mixes things up with different pipe layouts, so it doesn't get boring.

Great for when you're waiting around or just need to turn your brain off for a bit.
